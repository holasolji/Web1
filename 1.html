About 비타민찌

비타민찌 is a humanitarian aid worker, writer, traveler and Jiujitera.

비타민찌 has worked in sub-Saharan African countries and Latin American countries for 4 years. She has strong interest in women's empowerment projects in forced migration context, and she wants to contribute to bringing a better world where women and children no longer have to face SGBV and descrimination all across the world.

비타민찌 holds a Master of Science in International Humanitarian Action from the NOHA program where she was granted full scholarship from the EU Council as a part of Erasmus Mundus scholarship program. She is an author of the NY Times' best-selling book called "Living as a Female Aid Worker" and she is also a blogger of "Zzi, the Change-maker". She writes about her expereicnes living and working in hardship posts as a humanitarian worker and how it has affected her as a woman. Despite countless challenges, she still encourages many female humanitarian workers to join the field and never give up. Her stories have become a true inspiration for female aid practitioners.
